Cultural Preservation Crisis

Heritage Under Threat

Pollution, crowd pressure, careless development, neglect, and cultural loss can erase memory before people notice it is gone.

PollutionAir, water, and surface damage weaken stone, paint, metal, and fragile decorative details.
UrbanizationRapid growth pushes roads, crowds, noise, and construction pressure into older cultural zones.
NeglectSmall cracks, poor drainage, missing documentation, and weak maintenance become permanent loss over time.
Cultural lossLanguages, craft lineages, rituals, and oral histories fade when younger generations are not connected to them.

Damage comparison

The crisis is visible when we look closely

Threats are not abstract. They appear as cracks, stains, missing craft lineages, displaced communities, and traditions that no longer have time or space to continue.
