Technology and Preservation

Future of Heritage

Innovation can document, restore, teach, and protect culture when it serves communities instead of replacing them.

AI

Restoration Models

Machine learning can help identify damage, reconstruct missing visual patterns, and guide conservation planning.

AR

Layered Tourism

Augmented reality can reveal vanished structures, translated inscriptions, and historic scenes on-site.

VR

Virtual Museums

Immersive spaces allow students to visit fragile or distant sites without physical pressure on monuments.

3D

Drone Scanning

Photogrammetry and laser scanning preserve precise models for research, repair, and disaster recovery.

DB

Digital Archives

Open archives can store images, oral histories, maps, craft processes, and endangered language material.

IoT

Smart Protection

Sensors can monitor humidity, vibration, crowding, and pollution near sensitive heritage sites.

Technology should act like a careful archivist: precise, patient, and guided by the communities whose memory it protects.
